üìå What are Contests in Grand Arena Moku?

In Grand Arena, contests are the essential part of this fantasy gaming sport. They have multiple formats and are available periodically for a determined duration (we will talk about these specific details later).

Here is where you will try to use your cards in the best way possible to win rewards in USDC and mXp. This will be your objective throughout Season 1:

Get cards ‚Üí Build a lineup ‚Üí Enter a contest ‚Üí Earn rewards in USDC + mXp

üÉè Quick Overview of Cards

There are two types of cards: Champion Cards & Squeme Cards.

Champion Cards

Champion Cards have rarities, each with a multiplier:

  • Basic - 1.0x
  • Rare - 1.25x
  • Epic - 1.50x
  • Legendary - 1.75x
‚ùÆ ‚ùØ

Note: From my point of view, the rarity multiplier provides a big difference when using lineups of different rarities in the same contest, but choosing well which champion to place in your lineup is super important since you will rely on its performance in Moki Mayhem to climb the leaderboard.

Squeme Cards

Squeme Cards will give you an extra bonus depending on which Squeme Card you choose and which champions you include in your lineups.

You can learn more about this topic here: Grand Arena Wiki

To build a lineup, you will need 4 Champion Cards + 1 Squeme Card. The logic behind this is that champions compete at intervals while the contest is running; depending on the type of contest you enter, the champions will play a different number of matches, earn score points, and be positioned on a leaderboard within the contest where the rewards will be distributed.

‚öîÔ∏è Entering Contests

Once you have decided which lineup to use, you will go to the contests and see a lot of information, but don‚Äôt worry, we will explain it.

There are several types of contests. The number of participants will be determined in each contest and to enter you need to pay a fee using gems (100 gems, 200 gems, 300 gems, etc. remember that 100 gems ‚âà 1 USDC so please participate responsibly). You can also pay the entry fee with USDC.

‚ùÆ ‚ùØ
  • 50/50: If you finish in the top half of the leaderboard, you earn rewards (less risk)
  • Top 20%: You need to finish in the top 20% to earn rewards (higher risk)
  • Top 10%: You need to finish in the top 10% to earn rewards (much higher risk)
  • Free: These have no entry cost and unlimited entries. You can only earn mXp if you rank high (rewards will be shown in the contest details). Tip: you can use these to test lineups.

Contests can be weekly, daily, or half-day. Depending on the duration, this will determine the number of matches played by each champion. You will also find some contests limited by rarity, where you can only use the rarities allowed by the contest.

‚ùÆ ‚ùØ

Note: If you have several champions, diversify across different rarities so you can have them available for different contests.

üí∞ Contest Rewards

The main rewards in contests are:

  • USDC: In the contest details, you will find the amount you can earn. It will be deposited to your account once the contest ends.
  • mXp: You earn 50% of the coins spent on the entry fee, and if you win the contest and finish in the reward zone, you also earn mXp equal to 2/3 of the value of the gems won (remember 1 USDC = 100 gems, meaning if you win 3.50 USDC [350 gems] you will earn roughly 233 mXp).

üéØ How to Choose Champions for Your Lineup

The community has created different tools to track the performance of each champion. You can use tools like GA Tracker to analyze the meta, or see how they are performing on the leaderboard: Leaderboard Stats.

This will give you an idea of how each champion is performing over time and let you see their stats. You can use this information combined with the Squeme Card you choose to put your lineup into competition.

Once you are sure about a specific lineup, simply click the contest interface, select the Squeme Card you will use, and click "Enter Lineup".

Note: The (i) button contains all the info about the contest you are entering and the rewards you can earn.

Many players opt to use different strategies, such as using Squeme Cards like "DIVINE INTERVENTION" or "WHALE WATCHING" which grant points for using specific types of champions (e.g., Spirits or 1 of 1 respectively), or use other Squeme Cards that give points for certain champion actions in Moki Mayhem.

Tip: In the contest leaderboard, you can see which cards the top players are using and develop an idea of what the top players are choosing.
